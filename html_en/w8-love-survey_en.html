What is Love?

From Attraction to Letting Go

"Sometimes I wonder, we spend so much time learning skills, pursuing efficiency, understanding technology, yet we rarely spend time understanding—what is love."

We say "I like him," "I love her," but behind these feelings is a whole set of psychological processes. We are drawn by passion, yet tempered by reality; we seek intimacy, yet fear losing freedom.

This class invites you to explore five dimensions: the nature of love, relationship development, boundaries of respect, the language of communication, and the meaning of heartbreak. Because learning to love is not just about having someone, but about understanding ourselves more clearly. When we can understand our own ways of loving, we are less likely to get lost in love.

Getting to the Main Topic: "What is Love?"

Group Discussion Activity

Divide into 5 Groups

Start from your "personal experiences" and "situational assumptions," which will serve as a good foundation for using AI to find "objective definitions" and "cases" afterward.

Time Allocation

(Before grouping, spend 5 minutes organizing your thoughts: 20 minutes discussion, 5 minutes oral presentation, questions from each group.)

Discussion Focus

Answer the questions in the theme, explain your experiences and assumptions, the definitions and cases found through AI, and whether they support your experiences. What are the reasons?

【Theme 1: Multiple Facets of Love】

1a. (Definition) What do you think is the biggest difference between "love" and "liking"?

1b. (Scenario) Do you think "love at first sight" is more like "love" or "infatuation"? Why?

1c. (Importance) Why is it important to clarify "what my feelings for him/her really are"?

【Theme 2: Stages of Relationship Development】

2a. (Scenario) What do you find most enjoyable (or most troubling) about the "ambiguous" phase?

2b. (Process) Why do you think the feelings of the "honeymoon phase" (e.g., wanting to be together all the time) usually cannot last permanently?

2c. (Challenge) When the "adjustment period" appears (e.g., discovering the other person has flaws you cannot tolerate), do you think this is a sign of "incompatibility" or a "normal" process?

【Theme 3: Body Autonomy and Consent】

3a. (Definition/Boundaries) In daily life (not just in intimate relationships), what "boundaries" are important to you? (e.g., others touching your things without permission, looking at your phone)

3b. (Core Concept) Why is the phrase "silence does not equal consent" important?

3c. (Scenario) In what situations do you think we easily "assume" the other person has consented, but they may actually not have? (e.g., posting photos with friends on IG, ordering food for friends)

【Theme 4: Communication Skills for Healthy Relationships】

4a. (Scenario) When you are dissatisfied with a friend or partner, do you usually choose to "speak up directly" or "endure"? Why?

4b. (Accusation vs. Expression) Imagine a scenario: "The other person read your important message but didn't reply." What is the first "accusatory" thought that comes to mind? (e.g., You don't care about me!)

4c. (Skill) Following the above, if you were to turn this "accusation" into "expressing your feelings," how would you say it? (e.g., I saw you read it, but you didn't reply, and I'm a bit worried...)

【Theme 5: Coping with Breakup and Heartbreak】

5a. (Definition) Do you think "heartbreak" equals "failure"? Why?

5b. (Unhealthy Examples) What "unhealthy" ways of coping with heartbreak have you observed (or seen in movies/TV shows)?

5c. (Healthy Examples) What do you think is the one thing a friend "should do" and "should not do" to help someone who has just broken up?

Active Love

Psychologist Erich Fromm in "The Art of Loving":
"Love is not primarily a strong emotion, but an active decision and determination."

True Respect

"Is true respect waiting for someone to say no, or assuming from the start that they don't want to?"

Stability of Love

The stability of love comes from adaptation and adjustment, not from emotional highs and lows.

The Nature of Adjustment

Adjustment is not the fading of love, but love evolving from feeling to understanding.
The essence of adjustment is two worldviews realigning.

Losing a Relationship

When we lose a relationship, who exactly are we mourning?
Not just "him/her," but "myself in the relationship"
